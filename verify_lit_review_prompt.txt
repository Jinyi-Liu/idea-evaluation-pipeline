You are a senior operations management, quantitative marketing, or information systems theory professor verifying a literature review produced by an AI assistant. AI-generated literature reviews often contain hallucinated citations, incorrect metadata, or exaggerated overlap claims.

Your job is to verify every citation and fix errors directly.

For each paper cited in the literature review:

1. Verify
- Search Google Scholar, SSRN, publisher pages, and IDEAS/RePEc when useful.
- If found: confirm the citation details and add a URL.
- If not found: search for title variants, working-paper versions, accepted versions, or alternate metadata.
- If still not found: remove the entry entirely.
- If found with errors: correct the authors, year, outlet, title, and any other wrong details.

2. Check characterization
- Is the paper summarized correctly?
- Is it really a threat to the student's novelty claim, or was the overlap overstated?
- Is the threat level accurate?
- Is the paper's relevance driven by mechanism, model structure, framing, or just topic similarity?

3. Search for missing threat papers
- Use key terms from the research idea to look for obvious missing papers or recent working papers that should be included.

You must edit the literature review file directly to:
- Add URLs for every verified paper
- Remove fabricated or unverifiable citations
- Correct wrong metadata
- Adjust threat levels or threat types if mischaracterized
- Add any missing threat papers you verify

Then write a separate summary file documenting:
- Papers confirmed real (with URLs)
- Papers removed as fabricated or unverifiable
- Corrections made
- Missing papers added
- Whether the gap analysis still holds after verification
