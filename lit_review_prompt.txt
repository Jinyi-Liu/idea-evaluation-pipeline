Below is a theory-oriented research idea targeting premier operations management, quantitative marketing, or information systems journals. The student has already identified their 3 closest papers or models. Your job is to find papers they MISSED - immediate threats to novelty that are not already in their list.

Please do the following:

1. Threat search
Identify papers, models, and recent working papers that pose a direct threat to novelty. Focus on:
- Papers that use the same core mechanism or friction
- Papers with the same timing, information structure, contract form, or equilibrium logic
- Papers that generate the same qualitative comparative statics or substantive insight
- Adjacent-field papers that could make the contribution look incremental
- Recent working papers that appear to be running the same race

The goal is not to build a broad literature review. The goal is to surface the nearest competing papers that a referee would cite to challenge novelty.

2. Summarize and cite
For each threat paper, provide:
- Full citation (authors, year, outlet, title)
- URL (Google Scholar, SSRN, IDEAS/RePEc, conference page, or publisher link - you must include a verifiable URL)
- 2-3 sentence summary of how it specifically threatens the student's idea
- Threat level:
  - HIGH: overlaps core mechanism and core insight
  - MEDIUM: overlaps either mechanism or insight, but not both
  - LOW: not fatal, but a referee would likely mention it
- Threat type: mechanism threat, structure threat, framing threat, or race threat

Important: do not fabricate citations. Every paper must come from web search and include a real URL. If you cannot verify a paper, do not include it.

3. Gap analysis
Given the student's 3 cited papers and the new threats you found, assess:
- Is there still a defendable gap?
- What exactly survives as genuinely new?
- What is the single strongest argument that this is not just an incremental variant of an existing model?

4. Defensive recommendations
Suggest specific modifications to make the idea more robust against the threats identified. Recommend how to sharpen the framing, simplify the model, shift the benchmark, or recenter the mechanism.

Organize the output under clear headings such as:
- Student's Cited Papers
- New Threat Papers
- Gap Analysis
- Defensive Recommendations
